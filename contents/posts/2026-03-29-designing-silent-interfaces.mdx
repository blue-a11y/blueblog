Good interfaces do not ask for attention on every line.

They remove hesitation. They help people move. They stay out of the way until the moment detail matters.

When I design product surfaces, I start by cutting noise first. Visual contrast, spacing, copy density, and motion all need a reason to exist.

A page can be rich without feeling loud. That usually comes from structure, not decoration.
